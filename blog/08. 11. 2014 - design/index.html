Design State of Mind

What’s design? Why design?

People may consider it as painting, so that treat designers as painters or artists and stuff. From my perspective, that is partly right, but not precise and professional.

Design is not purely aesthetic, nor wholly analytical and functional. Sometimes it’s more appropriate to be called a holistic approach.

Firstly, I need to clear what my target is and what issunes there are. The next, find the best and most applicable way to solve those issues and the approach to my target.
In this process of thinking，the way of analysing, I think I’m in a design state of mind.

Design is about the way of thinking, the tools, the guidelines, the rational mind and data and so forth. As the way of thinking, when we think about design, what do we really think about? Logo? An innovative User Interface? A nice theme?
Actualluy it’s so much more than that.

  • Design is a method of problem solving. We choose different ways, like a clever UI/ an active motion/ blueprints/ a brochure or even a hammer, all we need is to solve a visual or physical problem.

  • The best designs are notorious for seeming not designed at all. What’s the good design? You get it when you can’t even see it.

  • When design, I need to think from these aspects: Is this product useful? Is the product understandable? Is my design unobtrusive? Is it long-lasting? Are there enough care/ accuracy and respect towards my consumers and partners? Does it concentrate on the essential aspects?

I always focus on accuracy / rational mind / simplicity / consideration and respect during my design process. I make a choice by observing users’ interaction and other good products, data and also my experiences.

Following the design guideline, which I treat as one of the most rare and greatest quality of a good & professional product designer，because it’s a kind of respect towards users in a platform and the guideline is also a conclusion made after thousands of times of rational analyses and experiments. Of course, I must understand every detail in this guideline.

Good design can’t be achieved with glossy buttons or masterful wireframes alone. It’s a merger of all these principles into something that is meaningful and deliberate.
